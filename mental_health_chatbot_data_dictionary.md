
# üßæ Data Dictionary: Mental Health Chatbot Diary Study

This dataset contains structured entries from participants reflecting on their use of mental health chatbots. Below are the variables, their definitions, and valid values (where applicable).

---

### `Name`
**Type:** String  
**Description:** Pseudonym used to identify the participant.  
**Example:** `"UserA"`

---

### `Date`
**Type:** Date (`YYYY-MM-DD`)  
**Description:** Date of the chatbot interaction.

---

### `chatbotUsed`
**Type:** String  
**Description:** Name and version of chatbot used during the interaction.  
**Examples:**  
- `ChatGPT (GPT-4o)`  
- `Claude 3`  
- `Mixed (GPT-5, GPT-4o)`

---

### `userSubscription`
**Type:** Categorical  
**Description:** The type of subscription the user had access to during the session.  
**Valid values:**
- `Free`
- `Paid`
- `Mixed (Free + Paid)`
- `Unknown`

---

### `interactionMode`
**Type:** Categorical  
**Description:** Mode(s) through which the chatbot was used.  
**Valid values:**
- `Text`
- `Voice`
- `Mixed (Text + Voice)`

---

### `interactionDuration`
**Type:** Numeric (minutes)  
**Description:** Estimated length of chatbot interaction. If participants gave qualitative ranges (e.g., ‚Äúover an hour‚Äù), numeric approximations were used (e.g., 75).  
**Examples:** `15`, `30`, `90`, `22.5`

---

### `usageReasons`
**Type:** Multi-select (list)  
**Description:** Reasons why the chatbot was used in that session.  
**Possible values:**
- `Emotional support (venting, feeling heard)`
- `Coping strategies (stress, anxiety, mood management)`
- `Relationship or social advice`
- `Self-reflection or journaling`
- `Curiosity/testing features`
- `Self-diagnosis`

---

### `locationUsed`
**Type:** Categorical  
**Description:** Where the participant used the chatbot.  
**Valid values:**
- `Home`
- `Work`
- `In Transit`
- `Outdoors`
- `Traveling`
- `Unknown`
- `Mixed (Home + Commuting)`, etc.

---

### `deviceUsed`
**Type:** Categorical  
**Description:** Device(s) used to access the chatbot.  
**Valid values:**
- `Laptop`
- `Smartphone`
- `Laptop + Smartphone`

---

### `socialUse`
**Type:** Categorical  
**Description:** Physical and social context during chatbot use.  
**Valid values:**
- `Alone (private)`
- `Alone (shared space)`
- `Alone (public)`
- `Alone (unspecified)`
- `Alone (private + public)`
- `Unknown`

---

### `Multi-context`
**Type:** Boolean (`Yes` / `No`)  
**Description:** Indicates whether multiple contexts (device, social setting, location) occurred in a single diary entry.

---

### `conversationDescription_user`
**Type:** String  
**Description:** Free-text summary of the conversation provided by the participant.

---

### `conversationDescription_LLM`
**Type:** String  
**Description:** Summary of the conversation as generated by the chatbot (via a prompt from the participant).

---

### `miscUserComments`
**Type:** String  
**Description:** Additional free-text comments provided by the participant.

---

## üîç Notes on Mixed Values

Where participants described multiple values for a single variable (e.g., used chatbot at home and while commuting), a `Mixed` label was applied along with specific details in parentheses:

**Examples:**
- `locationUsed = Mixed (Home + Commuting)`
- `userSubscription = Mixed (Paid ChatGPT + Free Gemini)`
- `socialUse = Alone (private + public)`

This approach preserves detail while maintaining consistent top-level codes.
